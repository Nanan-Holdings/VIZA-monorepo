## 1. Scope

This Policy describes how VIZA collects, uses, stores, shares, and
deletes Personal Information ("PII") in connection with the visa
application service operated at https://haggstorm.com (and successor
domains).

## 2. Categories of Personal Information collected

VIZA collects only what each destination country's visa application
requires.

| Category | Examples | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Identity | Full name, date of birth, sex, nationality | Applicant input |
| Contact | Email, phone, postal address, applicant inbox alias | Applicant input + assigned by VIZA |
| Travel document | Passport number, expiry, issuing country, machine-readable zone (MRZ) | Applicant upload (image OCR) |
| Photo | Passport-style photograph | Applicant upload |
| Supporting evidence | Itinerary, accommodation, employment, bank statements, where requested by the destination country | Applicant upload |
| Application answers | Country-specific form responses | Applicant input through the Companion |
| Government correspondence | Confirmation, decision, OTPs received at the applicant inbox alias | Inbound mail to haggstorm.com |
| Submission artefacts | Screenshots, run logs, captcha receipts | Generated by VIZA's automation |
| Payment | Stripe customer id, charge id, last-4 of card; we do not store full PAN | Stripe |

We do **not** intentionally collect special-category data (health,
biometrics beyond face photo, religion, sexual orientation, criminal
history) except where a specific country's application form requires
it. Where required, we surface the requirement explicitly and process
the data only for that submission.

## 3. Why we process it

- To complete and submit your visa application to the destination
  country's portal.
- To translate, normalise, and validate your inputs against the
  portal's expected format.
- To receive government correspondence on your behalf via the
  applicant inbox alias.
- To process payments for the agency fee and (where supported) the
  government fee.
- To generate audit and compliance records (including PII access logs
  required for incident response).

## 4. Legal bases (GDPR / equivalents)

- **Contract** (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR): processing is necessary to perform
  the visa application service you have requested.
- **Legal obligation** (Art. 6(1)(c)): retention of audit records.
- **Legitimate interest** (Art. 6(1)(f)): fraud prevention,
  abuse-resistance on the inbound mail layer, security audit logs.
- **Consent** (Art. 6(1)(a)): captured for marketing, optional
  research participation, and any processing not strictly necessary
  to perform the service.

## 5. Sharing — third-party processors (subprocessors)

We share PII only with the subprocessors enumerated in
[`subprocessors.md`](./subprocessors.md). At a glance:

- **Supabase** (Postgres + Storage + Auth) — primary data store.
- **Cloudflare** (Email Routing, R2, Workers, DNS) — inbound mail and
  attachment storage.
- **Stripe** — payment processing.
- **Anthropic** — AI Companion model inference.
- **2captcha** — solving image CAPTCHAs on government portals where
  unavoidable.
- **Government portals** — the destination country's official visa
  application service. We submit your application data to these as
  required to provide the service.

We do **not** sell PII or share it for advertising.

## 6. International transfers

VIZA is incorporated in Singapore. Data is stored on the
Asia-Pacific region of Supabase by default. International transfers
occur in two scenarios:

- **Provider footprint:** Cloudflare, Stripe, and Anthropic operate
  globally; data may be processed in their respective regions.
- **Destination portals:** by the nature of the service, your
  application data is submitted to the destination country's
  government, which constitutes a transfer to that country. You
  authorise this transfer when you authorise the submission.

## 7. Retention

Default retention windows are set in
[`docs/legal/retention.md`](./retention.md) (LEGAL-003) and enforced
by the SQL purge functions in
`viza-be/agent-backend/drizzle/0049_retention_purge.sql` (run on a
daily schedule via Supabase pg_cron). At a glance:

- Passport scans + photos: 12 months from submission completion.
- Submission artefacts (screenshots, run logs): 12 months.
- Application answers: 36 months unless deletion is requested earlier.
- Walker / recon captures: 90 days.
- Inbound mail received at the applicant alias: 180 days
  (configurable; see INBOX-007 retention policy).
- Audit logs (consent, PII access, secret access): 24 months.
- Payment metadata: 7 years (statutory).

Each scheduled purge writes a row to `retention_purge_log` so we can
prove the sweep ran. R2 / Storage objects are deleted by a follow-on
worker reading that log.

When you request deletion (LEGAL-004) we remove or pseudonymise PII
within 30 days, except where retention is legally required (e.g. tax
records on payments).

## 8. Consent capture

Each acceptance of these Terms / this Policy / a per-application
authorisation is recorded in `consent_event` with the SHA-256 hash of
the document version, the timestamp, requesting IP, and user-agent
(LEGAL-002). You may withdraw any non-required consent at any time
from `/client/account` settings.

## 9. Your rights

Subject to local law, you have the following rights:

- **Access** — download an export of your data via
  `/client/account/export`.
- **Rectification** — correct inaccurate data through the form UI or
  by emailing privacy@haggstorm.com.
- **Erasure / "right to be forgotten"** — initiate via
  `/client/account/delete`. A 7-day soft-delete window allows recovery
  if the request was accidental.
- **Restriction / objection** — email privacy@haggstorm.com.
- **Portability** — the export endpoint produces machine-readable
  JSON.
- **Withdraw consent** — for any consent-based processing.
- **Lodge a complaint** with your data protection authority. EU
  residents may complain to the supervisory authority of their
  habitual residence; UK residents to the ICO.

We do not use automated decision-making with legal effect. The
Companion makes recommendations; the human applicant decides whether
to submit.

## 10. Security

- Encryption at rest via Supabase / Cloudflare R2.
- Encryption in transit via TLS 1.2+ enforced.
- Per-applicant credential vault: portal passwords stored as
  AES-256-GCM ciphertext keyed off an env-side encryption key
  (SECRETS-001 → SECRETS-005).
- Pre-commit secret-scanning + audit log of every credential
  read/write/rotation.

## 11. Children

The service is not directed to children under 13. Applications on
behalf of minors are processed only when submitted by a parent or
legal guardian.

## 12. Changes

The current document hash is recorded in `consent_event` at sign-in.
Material changes are notified at next sign-in.

privacy@haggstorm.com — privacy or DSR requests
support@haggstorm.com — service questions
