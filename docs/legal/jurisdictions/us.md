# United States — state privacy laws

> **Status:** CPRA-aligned at platform level. State-level monitoring
> required (CPRA, VCDPA, CPA, CTDPA, UCPA, ...).

## Applicability

VIZA handles US residents' data through the US CEAC DS-160 runner and
when US residents apply for visas to other destinations. Federal
sectoral laws (HIPAA, GLBA) do not apply here; state comprehensive
privacy laws do above respective thresholds.

## Key obligations

| Obligation | VIZA posture |
|---|---|
| CCPA / CPRA (California) | Notice at collection, right to know / delete / correct, opt-out of "sale" or "sharing" — VIZA does neither. Privacy Policy §9 covers DSRs. |
| VCDPA (Virginia), CPA (Colorado), CTDPA (Connecticut), UCPA (Utah), TDPSA (Texas), and successors | Substantively similar to CPRA; rights are enumerated in the same Privacy Policy section. |
| Children — COPPA, plus state amplifications | We do not target under-13s; minors handled via guardian flow. |
| Breach notification | 50 different state laws. Standard practice: notify within state-specific windows (often 45–60 days) plus AG / consumer reporting agencies as required. |

## Gaps

- Annual right-to-know report cadence (CPRA).
- State-by-state breach notification matrix in the runbook.
- Opt-out preferences (Global Privacy Control) honour rule wired into the cookie/consent banner when added.
