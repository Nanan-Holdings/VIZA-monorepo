# EU Schengen Short-Stay (Type C) — FAQ

## Documents required

- Passport valid at least 3 months beyond intended stay.
- Photo 35×45 mm (413×531 px @ 300 dpi), light grey / off-white.
- Travel insurance covering at least €30 000 medical, valid across
  all Schengen states.
- Round-trip flight reservation.
- Accommodation booking covering the entire stay.
- Bank statements last 3 months (typically €60–80 per day).
- Employment / education proof.

## Processing time

- VIZA submission via France-Visas (or Italy-VFS-CN, depending on
  primary destination): ~1 hour after the consulate appointment is
  booked.
- Consulate processing: typically 15 calendar days, can extend to
  30 in busy season.
- Apply at least 6 weeks before travel.

## Refund eligibility

- Agency fee fully refundable until VIZA's runner reaches the
  payment step on the consulate portal.
- The Schengen fee (€90 adult / €45 child) is paid by the
  applicant on the consulate's payment page — VIZA does not
  handle it. Schengen fee is non-refundable on submission.

## Common rejection reasons

- Insufficient travel insurance — must explicitly cover all
  Schengen countries and €30 000 medical.
- Stated itinerary inconsistent with the country whose consulate
  you applied at (must be the country of longest stay or first
  entry).
- Insufficient evidence of return / ties to home country.
