- Learnings:
  1. The binary-PDF read path (WebFetch saves to tool-results/, then Read with `pages` parameter) is the canonical way to ingest government forms from inside this harness — WebFetch's HTML-only text extraction returns nothing useful on binary PDFs. Worth noting in the playbook as the preferred research-fallback flow.
  2. EU-level forms drift faster than expected — the 2019/1155 amendment (Feb 2020 in force) removed a whole repeatable field and added UK Withdrawal Agreement language. Any schema >3 years old should be re-verified against the current authoritative source, not against older reference implementations.

  5. Source-verification QA pass (authoritative PDF walk) is a meaningful partial for §3.1 — it catches static drift even when live-portal QA remains blocked by identity gates. Should be a standard step in the playbook for any country with a published form PDF.
