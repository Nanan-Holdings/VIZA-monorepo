### Learnings
1. Ant Design Vue's virtualized `.rc-virtual-list-holder` requires `aria-posinset` for dedup — counting visible DOM nodes across scroll passes double-counts because Ant recycles option divs as you scroll. aria-posinset is the stable key.
2. Ant's dropdown panels are reused across selects — clicking a new select before the previous panel is fully hidden gives stale options. Close-and-wait (Escape + waitForFunction(all .ant-select-dropdown-hidden)) between probes fixes it. The `aria-controls` attribute on the opener is the canonical link to the right panel.
3. The in-repo extension (v1.2.1) was a lossy approximation of the live form — it got ~39 of 60 fields right but invented 21 that didn't exist on live (sponsor branch, purpose sub-journeys, accompanying-adult block) and missed the children-table structure entirely. Live-portal recon is the only path to parity — extensions are a starting point, not a finish line.
4. The `live_dom_id` annotation pattern eliminates an entire mapping layer for downstream submission automation. If future countries have stable DOM ids (most Vue/React admin-form tools use deterministic keys like `basic_fieldName`), this should be the default — capture them in recon, store in validation_rules, reuse at submit time.
5. The v1 → v2 delta (81 → 60 fields, 10 → 9 steps) demonstrates that reconstructions systematically over-specify — it's safer to reconstruct generously and trim against live than to reconstruct minimally and miss fields. The downside is that trimming requires live access; the upside is nothing real gets dropped silently.
6. Vietnam's June 2025 province consolidation (63 → 34, Resolution 60/NQ-TW) is a recent policy change that the live form reflects but older docs don't — underscores the "re-validate quarterly" cadence. The DOM-id-keyed canonical.json from recon-v3 makes this a mechanical diff in future passes.
