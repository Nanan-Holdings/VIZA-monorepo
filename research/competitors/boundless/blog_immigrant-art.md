# These Notable American Artists Were Also Immigrants

One of the most famous American historical paintings, Washington Crossing the Delaware (1851), was created by German-born artist Emanuel Leutze, who immigrated to the U.S. as a child.

One of the most famous American historical paintings, of [George Washington Crossing the Delaware](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Washington_Crossing_the_Delaware) on the eve of the battles of Princeton and Trenton, was painted by the German-born artist Emanuel Leutze in 1851. Born in Wurtemberg in 1816, Leutze came to the United States as a child.

{{newsletter-component}}

Known for his 435-painting [_Birds of America_](https://www.audubon.org/birds-of-america), [John James Audubon](http://www.audubon.org/content/john-james-audubon) was born in 1785 on his father's sugar plantation in Saint-Domingue (today's [Haiti](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/by-country/haiti/)) and spent part of his childhood in Nantes, France before coming to America at age 18. Traveling with a set of paints, a gun, and an assistant, he spent the early 1820s traveling America to gather specimens, notes and sketches of American birds.

![Image 8](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68a3050860dd863f875cffa9/68c3bbd29f74cf7740bdfc72_image-26-700x475.png)

The Oxbow (The Connecticut River near Northampton) by Thomas Cole, 1836

The founder of the so-called "Hudson River School," [Thomas Cole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Cole) was born in England in 1801 and immigrated to the U.S. in 1818. His paintings of American wilderness landscapes inspired a generation of painters.

Also part of the Hudson River School, [Albert Bierstadt](https://americanart.si.edu/artist/albert-bierstadt-410) was born in Prussia (present-day Germany) in 1830 and came to the United States with his parents at age one. He was active in the Hudson River School and painted large-scale landscapes of the American West, especially the Sierra Nevada.

![Image 9](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68a3050860dd863f875cffa9/68c3bc0d1a90eb555fd1e2a0_image-27-700x481.png)

The Robert Gould Shaw Memorial, Boston Common (1884–1897) (cc) Carptrash

Sculptor [Augustus Saint-Gaudens](https://www.metmuseum.org/toah/hd/astg/hd_astg.htm) was born Ireland in 1848 to a French father and an Irish mother. His family came to the United States when he was just six months old. As a sculptor, Saint-Gaudens adopted a naturalistic style that departed from the staid neoclassicism that had been popular for decades. Saint-Gaudens' "Robert Gould Shaw Memorial" (1884–97) a frieze honoring a Civil War leader, is considered one of his masterpieces.

Abstract expressionist [Willem de Kooning was born in Rotterdam, the Netherlands in 1904.](https://www.moma.org/artists/3213)

[Mark Rothko](https://www.moma.org/artists/5047), a pioneer of so-called "color field painting," was born in the Russian Empire (present-day Latvia) in 1903, and immigrated with his family to the U.S. in 1913.

![Image 10](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68a3050860dd863f875cffa9/68c3bc25b300b9a66f22ed05_image-28-700x632.png)

Battle of Lights, Coney Island, Mardi Gras, by Joseph Stella, 1913–14

Modernist painter Joseph Stella was born in Italy in 1877. He came to the United States in 1896 as a medical student, but switched to painting. His works were displayed in the influential [1913 Armory Show](https://www.si.edu/spotlight/1913-armory-show).
