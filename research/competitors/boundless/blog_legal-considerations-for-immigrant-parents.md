# Legal Considerations for Immigrant Couples Expecting a Child in the U.S.

Check out some of these key legal considerations that all immigrant parents should be aware of in the U.S.

![Image 6](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68a3050860dd863f875cffa9/68c08b70caa9412fcd16a933_AdobeStock_168006944-couple-interview-scaled.jpeg)

Welcoming a child is an important life milestone for any couple, but for immigrant couples expecting a child in the United States, there are unique legal considerations to keep in mind. Navigating complex immigration laws, healthcare questions, and parental rights can be overwhelming. In this blog post, we’ll cover some of the most important legal considerations that immigrant couples should be aware of when expecting a child in the U.S.:

## Immigration Requirements and Citizenship Questions

One of the most important considerations for immigrant couples expecting a child in the U.S. is the child's [citizenship status](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/how-to-check-citizenship-status/). In the U.S., a child born on U.S. soil is generally considered a U.S. citizen, regardless of their parents' immigration status. This principle is known as "[birthright citizenship](https://www.uscis.gov/policy-manual/volume-12-part-h-chapter-3)."

If one or both parents are undocumented immigrants or in the U.S. on temporary visas, the child will still likely be considered a U.S. citizen if they are born in the country. However, it's essential to understand that this may not grant legal immigration status or other legal protections to the parents.

With this in mind, immigrant couples should be aware of any potential immigration consequences of having a child in the U.S. For example, if one parent is in the U.S. on a visa and gives birth, it's vital to understand how this might impact their visa status. Some visas may require notification to authorities when a child is born. Additionally, if a child chooses to sponsor their parents for immigration benefits after turning 21, it's essential to plan for potential delays or complications in the immigration process.

For more immigration help, Boundless put together a [U.S. immigration guide](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/help-with/children-immigration/) specifically for families and children.

## Healthcare Considerations

Access to healthcare during and after childbirth is a top priority for expecting couples, however navigating the U.S. healthcare system and its many legal requirements can be more complex for immigrants. For example, immigrant couples should be aware that some states and federal policies may restrict access to certain healthcare services for undocumented individuals or those on [nonimmigrant or temporary visas](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/temporary-u-s-visas-explained/). Therefore, it may be helpful to research available healthcare options and programs that can provide assistance regardless of immigration status.

Boundless put together a more detailed guide on navigating the U.S. healthcare system for immigrant parents [here](https://www.boundless.com/blog/us-healthcare-tips-for-immigrant-parents/).

## Utilizing Public Benefits

Immigrant families may have concerns about accessing public benefits to support their child's well-being, and it can be difficult for immigrant families to understand which types of programs they may qualify for. Eligibility for public benefits can vary based on immigration status, so it's important to understand which programs are available for immigrants and whether using certain benefits will have any impact on future immigration applications. Check out this [resource on utilizing public benefits](https://www.boundless.com/blog/public-charge-rule-explained/) to learn more.

## Child Custody and Parental Rights

Regardless of immigration status, when it comes to child custody and parental rights, it's important to establish clear documentation and legal guardianship arrangements to protect your child's best interests. In cases where one parent is not a U.S. citizen or has uncertain immigration status, it may be a good idea to consult an immigration attorney _and_ a family law attorney to create a plan that safeguards your child's future.
