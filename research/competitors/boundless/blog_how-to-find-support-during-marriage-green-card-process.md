# How to Find Support During the Marriage Green Card Process

We go over tips to help you during the visa process, including how to connect with other cross-border couples and join online communities.

![Image 6](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68a3050860dd863f875cffa9/68c3811fb1cc8d42898b8049_AdobeStock_112959735-scaled.jpeg)

Applying for a [marriage-based green card](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/marriage-based-green-cards-explained/) can be a long and difficult journey, both emotionally and logistically. It requires couples to navigate complex legal requirements and financial considerations, as well as manage the stress and uncertainty of the application process. It isn't easy to do all this without support, but fortunately, there are a number of ways for couples to find the help they need. In this article, we’ll explore some ideas for finding support during the marriage green card process.

## 1. Connect with other cross-border couples

One of the best ways to find support during the green card process is to connect with other couples who are going through the same thing. There are many online communities and social media groups for cross-border couples, where you can share experiences, ask questions, and find support. Some popular groups include the [VisaJourney](https://www.visajourney.com/) forum and the [LoveVisaLife](https://www.facebook.com/LoveVisaLife/) Facebook group. You can also search for local support groups or meetups in your area.

## 2. Seek counseling

![Image 8: A man in therapist's office](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68a3050860dd863f875cffa9/68c37a40b05357e11754dcd3_AdobeStock_211613751-1500x1001.jpeg)

The green card process can be stressful, and it’s important to prioritize your mental health during this time. Consider seeking counseling or therapy to help you cope with the emotional challenges of the process. A therapist can provide a safe space for you to talk about your feelings and concerns, and can offer strategies for managing stress and anxiety. If cost is a concern, look for low-cost or sliding-scale counseling services in your area, or consider online counseling options.

## 3. Get professional help with your application

Immigration lawyers can provide valuable guidance and support throughout the green card process. They can help you understand the legal requirements, navigate the application process, and advocate for your rights. Look for a lawyer with experience in marriage-based immigration cases, and don’t be afraid to ask for references or to schedule a consultation to discuss your case.

Or you can use a more affordable service like Boundless. We help you build a tailored visa plan for every step of the process, from forms to your immigration interview.

## 4. Use online resources

There are many online resources available to help you navigate the green card process. The U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) website is a great place to start — it provides detailed information about the application process and requirements. Boundless also has an extensive [library of resources](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/) about everything from the [timeline of a marriage green card](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/how-long-does-marriage-green-card-take/) to [what to expect at the interview](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/marriage-green-card-interview-explained/).

## 5. Lean on family and friends

![Image 9: Friends leaning on each other](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68a3050860dd863f875cffa9/68c3811eb1cc8d42898b8042_AdobeStock_177155087-1500x1000.jpeg)

Finally, don’t underestimate the importance of support from family and friends. Your loved ones can provide emotional support and help you manage the practical aspects of the green card process. Consider asking a trusted friend or family member to accompany you to appointments or to help you with paperwork.

The marriage green card process can be a challenging journey, but it doesn’t have to be a lonely one. By reaching out for support and using available resources, couples can find the help they need to navigate this complex process with confidence and peace of mind.
