The current total [wait time](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/average-green-card-wait-times) for a marriage-based green card averages about **8.2 months** (Boundless updates these figures monthly, based on analysis of USCIS data). ###### Important Note: Form I-130 Processing Time

All marriage green card applications begin with filing [Form I-130 (Petition for Alien Relative)](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/form-i-130-explained). The average processing time for Form I-130 is **around 14 months**. This estimate is based on analysis by Boundless partner [Track My Visa Now,](https://www.boundless.com/lp/importance-of-immigration-processing-times/)who tracks wait times in real time, giving the most up-to-date estimates for Form I-130 petitions filed today.

### When the spouse applying for a green card lives in the U.S. and is married to _a U.S. citizen_

*   Establishing the marriage relationship ([Form I-130](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/form-i-130-explained)) and applying for a green card at the same time ([Form I-485](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/form-i-485-explained)): 8.2 months

**Total estimated time:** 8.2 months ([full details here](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/guides/spouses-of-citizens-living-united-states))

### When the spouse applying for a green card lives in the U.S. and is married to _a U.S. green card holder_

*   Establishing the marriage relationship ([Form I-130](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/form-i-130-explained)) and after approval waiting until the visa bulletin has reached [the assigned priority date](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/how-to-read-the-visa-bulletin) to apply for a green card ([Form I-485](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/form-i-485-explained)): 35 months

**Total estimated time:** 35 months ([full details here](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/guides/us-family-spouse-noncitizen-us))

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

### Frequently Asked Questions

How much does the marriage green card process cost?

The cost of the marriage green card process can vary depending on a number of factors, such as whether the application is filed inside or outside of the United States. Right now, the application fee for a marriage green card is **$3005** for an applicant living in the United States or **$1340** for an applicant living outside the United States.

Note: In 2023, USCIS proposed a sweeping fee overhaul that could [increase the cost](https://www.boundless.com/blog/uscis-fees-to-increase/)for most immigration benefits substantially. [Learn how](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/uscis-gov-immigration-forms-and-fees/) your green card application could be impacted.

What are the requirements for a successful marriage green card application?

In order to successfully apply for a marriage green card, the applicant must be able to prove that their [marriage is genuine](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/how-do-we-prove-our-marriage-is-real/) and that they meet all of the [eligibility requirements](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/who-can-apply-for-a-marriage-based-green-card/). These requirements include being married to a U.S. citizen or permanent resident, being at least 18 years old, and not having any criminal history, among other factors.

What are some common reasons for delays in the marriage green card process?

There are a number of reasons why delays can occur during the marriage green card process. Some common reasons include problems with [proving the bona fides](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/how-do-we-prove-our-marriage-is-real/) of the marriage or issues with meeting the [eligibility requirements.](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/who-can-apply-for-a-marriage-based-green-card/) In recent years, [green card application processing times](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/average-green-card-wait-times/) have skyrocketed. Delays can also occur if there are errors in the application or if additional information is required by USCIS.

Can I work while my marriage green card application is pending?

If you are currently living in the United States and your marriage green card application is pending, you may be eligible to apply for [work authorization](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/form-i766-ead-card/). However, if you are applying for a marriage green card from outside of the United States, you [will not be able to work](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/working-in-the-united-states-while-waiting-for-your-green-card/) until you have received your green card and entered the United States.

Can I travel while my marriage green card application is pending?

If you are currently living in the United States and your marriage green card application is pending, you may be able to [travel outside of the United States](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/can-i-travel-while-my-adjustment-of-status-application-is-pending/) using what is known as advance parole. However, if you are applying for a marriage green card from outside of the United States, you will not be able to travel until you have received your green card and entered the United States.

What happens if my spouse dies while my marriage green card application is still pending?

Unfortunately, if your spouse dies during the pendency of your marriage green card application, it is possible your application will be revoked. However, for spouses of U.S. citizens, they may be able to continue with the green card process in specific circumstances. In this case, it is best to speak with an immigration attorney about your options.

How long does it take to become a U.S. citizen?

If you are already a permanent resident (green card holder), you can apply for citizenship after 5 years. If you are married to a U.S. citizen, you can apply for citizenship after 3 years. However, please keep in mind that these timelines are estimates and the actual time it takes may be shorter or longer depending on your individual circumstances.
