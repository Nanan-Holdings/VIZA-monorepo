## Additional textbooks to consider:

**Mae M. Ngai, _Impossible Subjects: Illegal Aliens and the Making of Modern America_**(Princeton: Princeton University Press, 2004)

 This textbook explores the origins and evolution of the concept of the "illegal alien" in U.S. immigration policy.

**Erika Lee, _America for Americans: A History of Xenophobia in the United States_**(New York: Basic Books, 2019)

 Lee offers a comprehensive history of xenophobia, connecting it to immigration patterns and policies.

**Adam Goodman, _The Deportation Machine: America's Long History of Expelling Immigrants_**(Princeton: Princeton University Press, 2020)

 Goodman provides an in-depth look at deportation policies and their effects on immigrant communities over time.

**Kelly Lytle Hernández, _City of Inmates: Conquest, Rebellion, and the Rise of Human Caging in Los Angeles, 1771–1965_**(Chapel Hill: University of North Carolina Press, 2017)

 This book details the intersection of immigration, incarceration, and racial politics in the history of Los Angeles.

**Natalia Molina, _How Race Is Made in America: Immigration, Citizenship, and the Historical Power of Racial Scripts_**(Berkeley: University of California Press, 2014)

 Molina explores how race has been constructed and redefined through immigration laws and practices.
