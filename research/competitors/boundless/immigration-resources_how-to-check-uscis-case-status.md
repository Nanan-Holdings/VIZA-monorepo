Learn how to keep up to date on the status of your case with USCIS

If you recently applied for a visa, green card, or citizenship with U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS), you’re likely eager to check on the status of your application.

### How Do I Check My USCIS Case Status Online?

1.   Have your [USCIS receipt number(s)](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/green-card-number-explained) on-hand
2.   Go to the government’s “Case Status Online” [tracker](https://egov.uscis.gov/casestatus/landing.do)
3.   Enter your receipt number(s)
4.   Select “Check Status”

Another easy way to check your USCIS case status is by visiting [myUSCIS](https://my.uscis.gov/) and creating an account. Once you have created an account, you can log in to see all of your pending applications or petitions. You will be able to view a complete list of your cases and their current statuses. If your USCIS case status is approved, you will be able to view the approval notice online.

![Image 8](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/689e09427059afed9bd7b4a5/68c182e6b6915db7203f52e8_annotation-info%20(STROKE).svg)

###### Important:

If you applied for an immigration benefit, such as a green card or citizenship, you will not be able to check your application status online until USCIS has received your application and sent you a [Notice of Action (Form I-797)](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/i-797-notice-of-action-explained).

## Understanding myUSCIS

myUSCIS is an official website of the U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS). It provides individuals with access to their immigration case information, applications and forms. myUSCIS allows you to:

*   Track your immigration documents
*   Receive notifications about upcoming events in your case, such as [interviews](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/us-visa-interviews-explained) or [biometrics appointments](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/biometrics-appointment)
*   Learn about your rights and responsibilities during the immigration process
*   Create a personal account where you can save important documents related to your case, request document translations and set up payment information for filing fees
*   Have access to all of your immigration information in one place, making it easier to manage the entire process

Additionally, myUSCIS provides information about USCIS services, local field office locations, and customer service options. It is useful for all visa applicants to stay up-to-date on their immigration case and ensure it remains on track.

For more information about myUSCIS or to create an account, visit the [myUSCIS homepage](https://my.uscis.gov/). You can also learn more about how to navigate the U.S. government’s various online immigration application systems [here](https://www.boundless.com/blog/navigating-us-immigration-electronic-application-systems/). Learn where to find your USCIS online account number [here](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/uscis-online-account-number-explained).

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

### USCIS Case Status FAQs

What visa types can you check your status online for?

You can check the status of your application online for many different visa types, including but not limited to [green cards](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/the-green-card-explained/), [family-based visas](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/complete-guide-to-family-based-immigration/), [employment-based visas](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/types-of-us-work-visas/) and other [temporary, nonimmigrant visas](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/temporary-u-s-visas-explained/).

How do I find my receipt numbers?

Your USCIS receipt number is a unique 13-character identifier that is assigned to you when you file certain forms with U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS). This number can be found on your [Form I-797, Notice of Action](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/i-797-notice-of-action-explained/), which is the form USCIS sends after it receives an application or petition from you. It can also be found at the top of your receipt when you pay for a filing fee or biometrics appointment. Additionally, you may find your USCIS receipt number by logging into your USCIS account using your username and password. Once logged in, you can view the details of any form you have submitted to USCIS, including the receipt number. You can also contact the [USCIS Contact Center](https://www.uscis.gov/contactcenter) for assistance in locating your receipt number.

Are there other ways to check my case status besides online?

You can also get a visa status check via email, mail, in-person, or over the phone. Learn more about these various methods [here](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/how-to-check-green-card-status/).

Can I contact USCIS directly?

For additional information about your case status, you can use the [USCIS Contact Center](https://www.uscis.gov/contactcenter). The Contact Center is available to answer any questions you may have and provide further assistance. If a decision has been made on your application or petition, they can tell you what it was and how to receive the decision notice. Boundless also put together some information on [how to contact USCIS directly](https://www.boundless.com/blog/3-quick-easy-ways-contact-uscis/).
