A [K-1 visa](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/k-1-fiancee-visa-explained), otherwise known as a fiancé visa, permits an engaged partner of a U.S. citizen to enter the United States. A K-1 visa holder must get married within 90 days of arriving in the country. Once married, they may then apply for a marriage green card.

In this guide, we’ll break down which documents you need for each stage of the application process.

## Full List of Required Documents

While every situation is different, in general, you’ll need the following documents when applying for a K-1 visa:

*   [Proof of citizenship](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/certificate-of-citizenship/#what-is-a-certificate-of-citizenship) for the U.S. citizen fiancé
*   Copy of sponsored fiancé’s passport
*   Evidence of [terminated marriages](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/get-copy-divorce-decree), where needed
*   Proof of meeting within 2 years of applying
*   Evidence of legal name change, where applicable
*   Sworn statements and evidence from both partners indicating their intent to marry and attesting to the legitimacy of their relationship
*   [A copy of Form I-94 arrival-departure records](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/form-i-94-travel-record-card-online/#h-where-to-get-a-copy-of-your-i-94), if applicable for sponsored fiancé
*   Evidence supporting [International Marriage Broker Regulation Act](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/imbra-explained) (IMBRA) waiver, if applicable
*   One passport-style photo for each fiancé
*   Two passport-style photos for the sponsored fiancé
*   [Birth certificate](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/how-to-get-a-copy-of-your-birth-certificate) for the sponsored fiancé
*   Valid, unexpired passport for sponsored fiancé
*   [Police clearance](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/obtain-police-clearance-certificate) obtained from countries where the sponsored fiancé lived for more than 6 months, including their country of residence
*   Sealed [medical exam](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/preparing-for-the-medical-exam) issued by approved physician, obtained by sponsored fiancé in their country of residence
*   Affidavit of Support ([Form I-134](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/form-i-134-explained)), submitted by U.S. citizen fiancé
*   Most recent tax returns of the U.S. citizen fiancé

## Documents Required for Online Nonimmigrant Visa Application (DS-160)

After the Petition for Alien Fiancé(e) is approved, the sponsored fiancé will need to submit [Form DS-160](https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/us-visas/visa-information-resources/forms/ds-160-online-nonimmigrant-visa-application.html). To do that, both fiancés will need to gather several items. Below are two lists — one for the sponsored fiancé and one for the U.S. citizen sponsor.

**DS-160 Checklist**

**Sponsored Fiancé:**

*   Valid, unexpired passport
*   Two passport-style photos
*   Birth certificate
*   Sealed medical exam given by an approved physician
*   [Police clearance](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/obtain-police-clearance-certificate) from all countries where applicant lived for more than 6 months, including their current country of residence

**U.S. Citizen****Fiancé**

*   Evidence of legitimate relationship, with the approved I-129F
*   Affidavit of Support (see next section for more details)
*   Most recent tax returns

The consular officer will use DS-160, and the answers provided at the interview, to determine whether the sponsored fiancé is eligible for a K-1 visa. In the final section, we’ll discuss which documents you need to bring to the interview.

## Documents Required for Affidavit of Support (I-134)

As part of the visa application process, the U.S. citizen sponsor will need to submit an Affidavit of Support stating they will be able to financially support their fiancé during their stay. When submitting [Form I-134](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/form-i-134-explained), the U.S. citizen sponsor will need to supply the following documents.

### I-134 Checklist

**Document Type**

**Required Documents**

Proof of Bank Account

Signed statement given by bank official indicating the date you opened the account, total deposits made within the past year, and current balance

Proof of Employment

Signed statement on business letterhead indicating your salary, the dates of your employment, the nature of your job, and whether you are a permanent or temporary employee

Proof of Federal Taxes

A copy of your most recent federal tax return, or, if you’re self-employed, a report of commercial rating concern

Proof of Bonds (if applicable)

List with denominations and serial numbers of bonds, and names of owners

## Documents Required for K-1 Visa Interview

Once form DS-160 is approved, the sponsored partner will need to [schedule an interview](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/k-1-visa-interview-explained) at their local embassy or consulate. In this section, we’ll go over which documents they need to bring to their appointment.

### K-1 Visa Interview Checklist

*   DS-160 confirmation page
*   Birth certificate
*   Police certificates for current country of residence and any country where sponsored fiancé(e) lived for more than 6 months
*   Evidence of financial support (the Affidavit of Support may be required)
*   Proof of relationship with U.S. citizen sponsor
*   Fee payment
*   Passport that will remain valid for at least 6 months after your arrival in the United States
*   Divorce or death certificates of previous spouses (for both fiancé(e)s)
*   Sealed medical exam
*   Two 2×2 photos

Many of these will seem familiar, as you and your partner have already collected this documentation when filling out the previous forms. So it’s important to keep all your documentary evidence organized throughout the K-1 visa application process.
