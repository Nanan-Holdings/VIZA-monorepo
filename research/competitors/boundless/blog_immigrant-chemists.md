![Image 8: Sir Fraser Stoddart](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68a3050860dd863f875cffa9/68c381110562b178555ac15b_image-150x150.jpeg)

**Sir Fraser Stoddart** shared the [2016 Nobel Prize in Chemistry](https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/chemistry/laureates/2016/) "for the design and synthesis of molecular machines." Born in Scotland, he has lived in the United States since taking a professorship at UCLA in 1997.

![Image 9: Lars Onsager](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68a3050860dd863f875cffa9/68c381110562b178555ac14c_image-1-150x150.png)

**Lars Onsager** won the [1968 Nobel Prize in Chemistry](https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/chemistry/laureates/1968/) "for the discovery of the reciprocal relations bearing his name, which are fundamental for the thermodynamics of irreversible processes." He was born in Norway and emigrated to the U.S. in 1928 to join the faculty of Johns Hopkins. He later taught at Brown and Yale.

{{newsletter-component}}

**Herbert C. Brown** shared the [1979 Nobel Prize in Chemistry](https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/chemistry/laureates/1979/) with Georg Wittig "for their development of the use of boron- and phosphorus-containing compounds, respectively, into important reagents in organic synthesis." He was born in London to Ukrainian Jewish immigrant parents. The family moved to Chicago when Brown was two years old.

![Image 10: Henry Taube](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68a3050860dd863f875cffa9/68c381110562b178555ac155_image-2-150x150.png)

**Henry Taube** won the [1983 Nobel Prize in Chemistry](https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/chemistry/laureates/1983/) "for his work on the mechanisms of electron transfer reactions, especially in metal complexes." Born in Saskatchewan, Canada, he came to the United States to earn his Ph.D. at Berkeley. He went on to work at Cornell, the University of Chicago and Stanford.

**Charles J. Pedersen** shared the [1987 Nobel Prize in Chemistry](https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/chemistry/laureates/1987/) with Donald Cram and Jean-Marie Lehn "for their development and use of molecules with structure-specific interactions of high selectivity." Pedersen was born in Busan, Korea in 1904 to a Norwegian father and a Japanese mother. In 1922 he came to the United States to study at the University of Dayton, Ohio. He worked as a chemist at Dupont for 42 years.

![Image 11: Walter Kohn](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68a3050860dd863f875cffa9/68c381110562b178555ac127_image-3-150x150.jpeg)

**Walter Kohn** won the [1998 Nobel Prize in Chemistry](https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/chemistry/laureates/1998/) "for his development of the density-functional theory." He was born in Vienna, Austria and escaped from Nazi rule as part of the Kindertransport rescue operation. He spent time as a refugee in England and Canada. He earned his Ph.D. in physics at Harvard in 1948.

![Image 12: Martin Karplus](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68a3050860dd863f875cffa9/68c381110562b178555ac152_11253406456_a83f6a0170_z-150x150.jpeg)

**Martin Karplus** shared the [2013 Nobel Prize in Chemistry](https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/chemistry/laureates/2013/) with Michael Levitt and Arieh Warshel "for the development of multiscale models for complex chemical systems." He was born in Vienna, Austria and educated at Harvard and at Caltech, where he completed his Ph.D. under Linus Pauling.

![Image 13: Rudolph A. Marcus](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68a3050860dd863f875cffa9/68c381110562b178555ac139_image-4-150x150.jpeg)

**Rudolph A. Marcus** won the [1992 Nobel Prize in Chemistry](https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/chemistry/laureates/1992/) "for his contributions to the theory of electron transfer reactions in chemical systems." He was born in Montreal, Quebec and became a U.S. citizen in 1958.

![Image 14: Ahmed Zewail](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68a3050860dd863f875cffa9/68c381110562b178555ac15e_image-5-150x150.jpeg)

**Ahmed Zewail** won the [1999 Nobel Prize in Chemistry](https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/chemistry/laureates/1999/) "for his studies of the transition states of chemical reactions using femtosecond spectroscopy." He was born in Egypt and moved to the U.S. for graduate studies at the University of Pennsylvania

![Image 15: George Andrew Olah](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68a3050860dd863f875cffa9/68c381110562b178555ac158_image-6-150x150.jpeg)

**George Andrew Olah** won the [1994 Nobel Prize in Chemistry](https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/chemistry/laureates/1994/) "for his contribution to carbocation chemistry." He was born in Budapest, Hungary and became a U.S. citizen in 1971

![Image 16: Venkatraman Ramakrishnan](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68a3050860dd863f875cffa9/68c381110562b178555ac12e_image-7-150x150.jpeg)

**Venkatraman Ramakrishnan** shared the [2009 Nobel Prize in Chemistry](https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/chemistry/laureates/2009/) "for studies of the structure and function of the ribosome." He was born in Chidambaram, India and obtained his Ph.D. from Ohio University in 1976

![Image 17: Arieh Warshel](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68a3050860dd863f875cffa9/68c381110562b178555ac132_image-8-150x150.jpeg)

**Arieh Warshel** shared the [2013 Nobel Prize in Chemistry](https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/chemistry/laureates/2013/) "for the development of multiscale models for complex chemical systems." He was born in British Palestine (now Israel) and initially came to the U.S. to do postdoctoral work at Harvard.

![Image 18: Alan MacDiarmid](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68a3050860dd863f875cffa9/68c381110562b178555ac13c_image-9-150x150.jpeg)

**Alan MacDiarmid** shared the [2000 Nobel Prize in Chemistry](https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/chemistry/laureates/2000/) "for the discovery and development of conductive polymers." Born in New Zealand, he came to the U.S. on a Fulbright Fellowship in 1951 and spent 45 years on the chemistry faculty of the University of Pennsylvania.

![Image 19: Yuan T. Lee](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68a3050860dd863f875cffa9/68c381110562b178555ac12a_image-10-150x150.jpeg)

**Yuan T. Lee** shared the [1986 Nobel Prize in Chemistry](https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/chemistry/laureates/1986/) with Dudley Herschbach and John Polanyi "for their contributions concerning the dynamics of chemical elementary processes." Born in Taiwan, he earned his Ph.D. at U.C. Berkeley in 1965.

![Image 20: Aziz Sancar](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68a3050860dd863f875cffa9/68c381110562b178555ac143_image-11-150x150.jpeg)

**Aziz Sancar** shared the [2015 Nobel Prize in Chemistry](https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/chemistry/laureates/2015/) "for mechanistic studies of DNA repair." He was born in Turkey and received his Ph.D. at the University of Texas at Dallas.

![Image 21: Michael Levitt](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68a3050860dd863f875cffa9/68c381110562b178555ac14f_image-12-150x150.jpeg)

**Michael Levitt** shared the [2013 Nobel Prize in Chemistry](https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/chemistry/laureates/2013/) "for the development of multiscale models for complex chemical systems." Born in Pretoria, South Africa, he lived in Israel before moving to the U.S. to teach at Stanford.

![Image 22: Roald Hoffmann](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68a3050860dd863f875cffa9/68c381110562b178555ac140_image-13-150x150.jpeg)

**Roald Hoffmann** shared the [1981 Nobel Prize in Chemistry](https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/chemistry/laureates/1981/) with Kenichi Fukui "for their theories, developed independently, concerning the course of chemical reactions." Born in an area of Poland that is now part of Ukraine, Hoffman lost most of his family in the Holocaust. He emigrated to the U.S. in 1949.
