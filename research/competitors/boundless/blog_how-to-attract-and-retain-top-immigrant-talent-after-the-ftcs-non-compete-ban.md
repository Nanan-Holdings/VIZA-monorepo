# How to Attract and Retain Top Immigrant Talent After the FTC's Non-Compete Ban

Attract top immigrant talent in the post-non-compete era. HR strategies for recruiting, retaining, and leveraging a more mobile workforce.

![Image 6](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/68a3050860dd863f875cffa9/68c37a246255a1cba0eb4099_AdobeStock_207650278-scaled.jpeg)

The Federal Trade Commission’s (FTC) recent ban on non-compete clauses marks a significant shift in the talent landscape, particularly for HR professionals supporting immigrant workers. While this decision opens up new possibilities, it also challenges companies to prioritize retention in a more competitive market.

## Navigating the Double-Edged Sword

The FTC’s move empowers immigrant workers to move more freely between companies, creating a wider talent pool for HR teams. However, this newfound mobility also means businesses must emphasize loyalty and engagement to retain their top talent.

### How HR can adapt

HR professionals can proactively address this changing environment by:

*   Revising employee value propositions: Ensure compensation and benefits packages are competitive and align with industry standards. Consider unique benefits appealing to immigrant workers.
*   Strengthening career development programs: Provide mentorship, training, and clear paths for advancement within the company.
*   Fostering inclusive work environments: Prioritize diversity and inclusion initiatives that go beyond basic training and make all employees feel welcome.

### Understanding the impact

Historically, non-compete clauses were common in the U.S., with estimates suggesting up to 46% of workers were subject to them. Foreign workers in high-skill industries like technology were particularly likely to be affected. The FTC’s ban will lead to increased talent mobility, potentially impacting timelines for Green Cards and other immigration processes.

### Retention Takes Center Stage

HR must go beyond attracting immigrant talent and focus on creating workplaces where they want to stay. This includes:

*   Competitive compensation and benefits: Pay must be fair, and benefits should include options like language classes, family immigration benefits, or relocation support.
*   Fostering a culture of support and growth: Immigrant employees need to feel valued and have opportunities to develop professionally. Building an immigration program into your company culture can enhance the experience for every employee on the team.

Embracing the benefits

Access to a Wider Talent Pool: The ban allows companies to recruit skilled immigrant workers previously restricted by non-competes.

Boosting Diversity and Innovation: Research consistently shows that diverse workforces drive innovation and problem-solving.

### Strategies for Success

1.   ︎Adaptable Immigration Processes
    *   Streamline sponsorship procedures and stay up-to-date with changing regulations to ensure smooth transitions for both incoming and departing immigrant talent.

2.   Emphasizing Employee Engagement
    *   Offer professional development and mentorship, and create a sense of belonging to build loyalty and reduce the desire for immigrant employees to look elsewhere.

### The Bottom Line

The FTC’s non-compete ban offers HR teams both challenges and opportunities in managing immigrant workers. By understanding the impact, prioritizing retention, and embracing diversity, organizations can successfully navigate this new landscape and position themselves to attract and retain the best immigrant talent.
