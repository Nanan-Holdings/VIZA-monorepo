If you’re considering a [K-1 fiancé visa](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/k-1-fiancee-visa-explained) to join your U.S. citizen partner, it’s important to understand its advantages. This guide focuses specifically on the benefits of the K-1 visa and how it compares to other visa options. While we won’t cover the full application process here, we’ll help you understand why the K-1 visa might be the right choice for your situation.

###### What is the K-1 Fiancé Visa?

The K-1 visa allows the non-U.S. fiancé of an American citizen to enter the United States to get married. Couples must marry within 90 days of the non-citizen partner’s arrival.

## Top 5 Benefits of the K-1 Visa

1.   **Fast Process**: It usually takes 8-11 months from application to approval. This is quicker than many other visas. Learn more about K-1 processing timelines [here](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/how-long-does-k-1-visa-take).
2.   **Marry in the USA**: You can have your wedding anywhere in America. Big city or small town, beach or mountains – it’s your choice.
3.   **Work Permission**: Once the K-1 visa holder files to adjust their status, they can also request [travel documents](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/the-advance-parole-travel-document-explained) and [work permits](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/form-i766-ead-card) while the green card request is processing.
4.   **Faster Path to Green Card**: After marriage, you can apply for a [green card](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/the-green-card-explained) without leaving the USA. Many documents you used for the K-1 visa will help with this process.
5.   **Open to All Couples**: [Same-sex couples](https://travel.state.gov/content/dam/visas/DOMA/DOMA%20FAQs.pdf) can use the K-1 visa too. This is very helpful if your home country doesn’t allow same-sex marriage.

## K-1 Visa vs. Other Options

### K-1 Visa or Marriage Visa (CR-1/IR-1)?

Both lead to a green card, but there are important differences:

*   **Speed**: K-1 is usually faster (6-9 months vs. 12-18 months for marriage visas).
*   **When to Marry**: For K-1, marry after U.S. entry. For CR-1/IR-1, marry before applying.
*   **Work Status**: K-1 holders apply for work permission after arrival. CR-1/IR-1 holders can work immediately upon arrival.
*   **Travel**: CR-1/IR-1 holders can easily travel in and out of the U.S. K-1 holders need extra paperwork to travel after entry.

Learn more about the pros and cons of K-1 Fiancé visas vs. Spousal Visas [here](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/how-is-a-fiance-visa-different-from-a-marriage-based-green-card).

### K-1 Visa vs. Family Visas

The K-1 is specifically for fiancés. Other family visas are for different relationships:

*   **Immediate Family Visas**: For spouses, parents, and unmarried children under 21 of U.S. citizens.
*   **Extended Family Visas**: For other relatives or family of green card holders.

K-1 visas are often faster than many extended family visa options.

## Additional Benefits to Consider

1.   **Benefits for Children**: If you have unmarried children under 21, they may be eligible for K-2 visas to join you in the USA.
2.   **Simpler Adjustment of Status**: After marriage, the process to get a green card (known as adjustment of status) is often smoother for K-1 visa holders compared to other visa types.
3.   **Potentially Lower Overall Costs**: Government fees for a K-1 visa can be higher than those for married-couple visas, but overall costs might be lower, especially when factoring in travel and time expenses associated with multiple trips abroad for a spousal visa process.
4.   **Cultural Transition**: Entering the U.S. before marriage allows you time to adjust to American culture and your new life together before taking the big step of marriage.
5.   **Interview Process**: The K-1 visa interview is often less intensive compared to some other immigration interviews, as the focus is primarily on proving the legitimacy of your relationship.

## Important Things to Know About the K-1 Visa

Here are a few things to keep in mind when considering whether or not to [apply for a K-1 visa](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/k-1-fiancee-visa-explained):

1.   :[**90-Day Rule**](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/90-day-rule-explained) You must marry within 90 days of arriving in the USA.
2.   **Meeting Requirement**: You must have met in person within two years before applying. There are some exceptions.
3.   **Financial Needs**: The U.S. citizen must prove they can [support you financially.](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/k-1-fiancee-visa-explained/#k1-visa-eligibility)
4.   **One Chance**: You can only marry the U.S. citizen who sponsored your K-1 visa.
