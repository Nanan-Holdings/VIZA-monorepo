The outbreak of the novel coronavirus has been declared a pandemic by the World Health Organization (WHO). Numerous countries, including the United States, have implemented travel or entry restrictions at their ports of entry. These changes will impact nonimmigrant visa holders as well as certain immigrants. The outbreak has also [disrupted immigration services](https://www.uscis.gov/about-us/uscis-office-closings) and proceedings within the United States.

*This page is updated regularly with information about COVID-19 and its effect on U.S. immigration.

**April 29**

*President Biden is expected to [ban most travel](https://www.reuters.com/world/us/exclusive-biden-set-ban-most-travel-us-india-limit-covid-19-spread-2021-04-30/) from India to the United States starting Tuesday to curb the spread of COVID-19. India is currently grappling with a severe outbreak of the virus.

**April 28**

*Learn which U.S. embassies and consulates [have reopened](https://www.boundless.com/blog/covid-19-u-s-embassy-and-consulate-reopening/) amid the coronavirus pandemic.

**March 25**

*USCIS announced it would again extend the response deadlines for Request for Evidences (RFEs) and Notices of Intent to Deny (NOIDs) due to the coronavirus pandemic. If an RFE or NOID was issued between March 1, 2020 and June 30, 2021, applicants now have an additional 60 days beyond the original deadline to respond to USCIS.

**Feb. 24**

*President Biden issued an executive order to lift the immigrant visa ban, allowing family members of U.S. citizens and green card holders to join their families in the United States. Learn more [here](https://www.whitehouse.gov/briefing-room/presidential-actions/2021/02/24/a-proclamation-on-revoking-proclamation-10014/).

**Feb. 10**

*The Biden administration [announced](https://www.law360.com/articles/1354638/state-dept-exempts-foreign-students-from-travel-restriction) that students from Europe with current visas will be exempt from COVID-19 travel restrictions.

**Jan. 25**

*President Biden [issued an executive order](https://www.whitehouse.gov/briefing-room/presidential-actions/2021/01/25/proclamation-on-the-suspension-of-entry-as-immigrants-and-non-immigrants-of-certain-additional-persons-who-pose-a-risk-of-transmitting-coronavirus-disease/) extending a previous suspension of all non-U.S. citizens from the United Kingdom, Ireland, Schengen countries and Brazil from entering the United States. South Africa was also added to the list.

**Jan. 24**

* The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) is requiring negative covid tests from all individuals entering the United States starting January 26, 2021.

**Jan. 4**

*Trump extends his proclamations limiting immigration into the United States until March 31, 2021. The proclamation _does not_ apply to people filing green cards from within the United States ("[adjustment of status](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/adjustment-status-process-explained/)").

**Dec. 21**

*Households with family members of mixed immigration status [will be eligible](https://www.usnews.com/news/national-news/articles/2020-12-21/households-with-mixed-immigration-status-eligible-for-stimulus-checks-under-new-coronavirus-bill) for coronavirus stimulus payments as part of the new COVID-19 relief package expected to pass Congress on Monday. The previous relief package in March excluded payments to undocumented immigrants and their families, as well as to those who were in the country legally but did not qualify for a Social Security Number.

**Nov. 17**

*DOS updated its announcement and FAQs on the phased resumption of visa services, noting that it will **resume routine visa services on a post-by-post basis**, but is unable to provide a specific date for when each mission will resume specific visa services, or when each mission will return to processing at pre-COVID levels. DOS also announced that it has extended the validity of Machine Readable Visa (MRV) fees until December 31, 2021, to allow all applicants who were unable to schedule a visa appointment due to the suspension of routine consular operations an opportunity to schedule and/or attend a visa appointment with the fee they already paid.

**Nov. 6**

*Read this [comprehensive report](https://www.americanimmigrationcouncil.org/research/impact-covid-19-us-immigration-system) from the American Immigration Council on the impact of the coronavirus on immigration and non-U.S. citizens.

*The Trump administration announced earlier this week that it would impose further restrictions on the H-1B work visa program. USCIS said in a statement that the move would ensure jobs go to American workers amid the coronavirus pandemic.

**Sept. 21**

*USCIS will continue to allow green card applicants to respond to [Requests for Evidence](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/what-is-a-request-for-evidence-rfe-and-what-should-i-do-about-it/) (RFE) within 60 days of receiving one. The extension will apply to requests dated between March 1 and September 11.

**Sept. 15**

*An appeals court paused an injunction [that temporarily halted the public charge rule](https://www.nilc.org/2020/07/29/injunctions-block-public-charge-rules-amid-pandemic/) from being enforced while the country is under a national public health emergency due to COVID-19. The Department of Homeland Security (DHS) [is likely to re-impose](https://twitter.com/doug_rand/status/1305142525301268480) the rule nationwide.

**Sept. 11**

* The coronavirus pandemic has disproportionately affected immigrant workers, and [according to new data](https://www.boundless.com/blog/coronavirus-immigrants-job-losses/), could significantly slow down America's economic recovery.

*A federal judge has [temporarily barred](https://www.law360.com/articles/1307817) the Trump administration from applying its travel ban to winners of the [Diversity Visa Lottery](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/diversity-visa-lottery/), finding that the ban is possibly unlawful.

**Aug. 31**

Boundless can help you complete your [**marriage green card application**](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/marriage-based-green-cards-explained/) or your[K-1 visa **application**](https://www.boundless.com/immigration-resources/k-1-fiancee-visa-explained/)**.** We stay with you right up until the finish line when your visa gets approved. [Get started today](https://explore.boundless.com/)!

USCIS Deputy Director for Policy Joseph Edlow said in a statement that aggressive spending cuts will "increase backlogs and wait times across the board, with no guarantee we can avoid future furloughs," adding that congressional funding will be necessary to keep the agency afloat in 2021.

The decision comes after processing delays of Employment Authorization Documents due to the coronavirus pandemic.

*USCIS is planning to furlough two-thirds of its staff at the end of the month, which could "[grind [legal immigration] to a halt](https://www.forbes.com/sites/stuartanderson/2020/08/17/uscis-staff-furloughs-will-grind-legal-immigration-to-a-halt/#4b8d756b1152)."

The decision follows an [earlier ruling](https://www.nilc.org/2020/07/29/injunctions-block-public-charge-rules-amid-pandemic/) that temporarily halted the rule from being enforced nationwide while the United States is under a national public health emergency due to the coronavirus pandemic.

**Aug. 11**
